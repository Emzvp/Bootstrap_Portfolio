About me

Emily is currently at the Rutgers Coding Bootcamp in Somerset, New Jersey. She graduated Magna Cum Laude from Seton Hall University in 2018 with a bachelors in English and Political Science. While attending Seton Hall she was an active member in her business fraternity, Alpha Kappa Psi, acting as the event coordinator, utilizing her eye for aestetics and design. She was also a writer and editor of three student run newspapers: The Stillman Exchange, The Setonian, and The Diplomatic Envoy. In 2016 she received first place from the New Jersey Collegiate Press Association in critical writing for her controversial piece on honor killings in the middle east. Additionaly acknowledged was her article on the political opinion in Cuba, written on location during a month of research in the country.


When Emily is not working or in school she enjoys to spend her time writing, reading, snowboarding, playing chess, cooking and traveling. She became passionate about experiencing other cultures, having never left the continent, and since 2014 has visited 28 countries. A few months in South East Asia and South America was her inspiration behind her blog 196Plates, focusing on recreating recipes from all the countries she has traveled to. In 2019 this developed into a Meal Prep company, bringing healthy diverse international cuisine to customers in her community.
